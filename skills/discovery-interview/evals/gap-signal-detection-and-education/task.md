# Fitness App Discovery — Interview a Non-Technical Founder

## Background

A first-time founder has approached you wanting to build a fitness tracking app. She has a clear vision in her head, but her background is personal training, not software. She sent you this brief before your session:

> "I want to build a fitness app where personal trainers can create workout plans for their clients. Clients follow the plans on their phones and log their sets and reps. Trainers can see progress. I'm thinking a slick mobile app, maybe with a dashboard for trainers on the web. We'll add millions of users once it takes off — it should be able to scale. There's really just basic login, nothing complicated. I want to use MongoDB because my friend said it's perfect for apps like this. Integrations will be simple — maybe Stripe at some point for billing, but that's straightforward to add."

The founder is enthusiastic but has made several statements that reveal common blind spots: she's describing the solution rather than the problem in places, hasn't thought through the actual user journey, is assuming scale without understanding infrastructure implications, is treating security as trivial, and is picking technology based on hearsay without understanding tradeoffs.

## Your Task

Conduct a discovery interview with this founder to gather the information needed to write a thorough product specification. Since this is a simulated session, you will both ask the questions and answer them based on the brief — filling in reasonable assumptions where gaps exist, and explicitly flagging where you are making assumptions.

Produce two output files:

1. **`interview_log.md`** — A complete record of the discovery interview. For each question asked, record: the question, the category it addresses, and the answer (or assumption made). Flag anywhere the founder's original brief contained a blind spot or gap that your questions surfaced, and note how you corrected or educated on it.

2. The final specification document saved to an appropriate location in the workspace.

Focus on covering all major product areas, not just the surface features. The interview should surface the things the founder hadn't thought about yet.
